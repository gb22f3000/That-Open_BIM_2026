## Generative Design in Autodesk Fusion

Generative design uses algorithms and AI to explore design solutions based on criteria and constraints.

### Workflow
1. Define design problem (materials, manufacturing, performance, constraints)
2. AI generates design alternatives
3. Evaluate for performance, manufacturability, sustainability

### Key AI Features
| Feature | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| **Generative Design Clustering** | Groups similar design outcomes using ML |
| **Generative Design Optimization** | Refines performance and manufacturability |
| **Recommendation Engine** | Personalized design suggestions based on criteria |

### Benefits
- Enhanced decision-making and efficiency
- Superior performance and cost efficiency
- Innovative design exploration
- Improved collaboration and communication
- Personalization and continuous improvement

## AI's Paradigm Shift in Architectural Practice

### Threats & Opportunities
- Initially threatens entry-level architect roles
- Ultimately **changes** the architect's role rather than replacing it

### Key Impacts
1. **Improved design quality** — pushes boundaries, refines decisions during sketch design (not just later stages)
2. **Better visualization** — realistic images/walkthroughs with design controls, zoning, environmental factors simulated early
3. **Faster augmentation** — reduces dependency on parallel specialist teams
4. **Precise optimization** — statistical, cultural, political, social, ethical, multidisciplinary aspects via trained datasets
5. **Reduced collaboration overhead** — AI helps curate optimal designs from many options

### Design Phase Changes
- Iterative best-solution evaluation at ideation phase
- Automated repetitive tasks
- Data-driven design insights
- Inclusive and equitable decision-making
- Streamlined design-to-construction process
